GENESIS / ORIGIN

RATIO was not born from a theory.
It was born from a real problem.

Over the years, Cristiano directly observed how many of the most costly decisions for an organisation do not stem from a lack of technical skills, but from decision-making errors in high-impact hiring.

Organisations invest significant resources in selection, but often rely exclusively on CVs, personal impressions or unstructured intuition.

Projects slowed down or stalled
Strategic opportunities missed
Destabilised teams
Performance below expectations
THE RATIO THESIS

The goal is not to evaluate people.
The goal is to protect decisions.

A distinction that completely changes the point of observation, the tools used and the output delivered to the decision maker.

THE CONTEXT

Remote work rarely creates problems. It exposes them. Decisions hidden in DMs, undocumented assumptions, reactive communication — they existed long before. Distance simply makes the cost visible.

The strongest organisations don't rely on proximity. They rely on clarity, shared ownership and making the right information visible to the right people.

METHOD / APPROACH

Decision Intelligence
applied to executive hiring

Organisations don't need to eliminate every risk. They need to reduce avoidable errors. Better decisions don't come from intuition — they come from better information.

PRINCIPLE 01

No risk is zero

The goal is not to eliminate uncertainty. It is to reduce the errors that could have been avoided with better information and a more rigorous process.

PRINCIPLE 02

Information changes outcomes

Every executive hire is a high-impact decision. The difference between an outstanding and a disastrous outcome is often not the candidate. It is the quality of the analysis that precedes it.

PRINCIPLE 03

The process is the advantage

A structured decision framework reduces the impact of cognitive bias, standardises evaluation and produces outputs that are defensible in front of the board.
